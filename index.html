Tank Identification Guide

Identification Tips

  • Look at the silhouette and overall shape
  • Check the turret design and gun barrel
  • Observe the suspension and track system
  • Note the engine deck and exhaust
  • Pay attention to the commander's cupola
  • Count the road wheels and their spacing
  • Identify the main gun caliber and length
  • Look for distinctive markings or camouflage
  • Check the presence of side skirts or armor
  • Note the overall size and proportions

Essential Fundamentals

Tanker Roles

Understanding the roles and who does what

Tank Commander - The "Squad Leader" of the tank crew. This individual can spot and mark things on the map. They can play any role in the tank, but is recommended to play as the spotter as they are typically pre occupied by communicating to other squad leaders on the team & marking targets on the map. The Tank Commander role has two sub classes. One with a sub machine gun and one with a pistol and repair torch. Personally, I recommend the submachine gun as it's able to combat most satchels if timed correctly.
Tank Crewman - The tank crewman are simply the "other two" squad mates in a tanker squad. These crewman can also play any role in the tank however it is recommended that they play as driver or gunner. This said, there is no true method these are simply recommendations. You should play whatever role you and your tank mates decide as teamwork and comfortability is the ultimate deciding factors. This role comes with two sub classes. The starting unit comes with no repair torch, and the second one (once you earn level three) gets the repair torch. In ever single scenario you should always equip the repair torch class.

Health Management

Monitor all four health bars - each affects combat capability in critical ways.

Hull

This is considered the overall health of the tank. Use this to gauge how much health your vehicle has.

Turret

A damaged turret will slow the rotation of the gun, as well as prevent the use from your coaxial machine gun if your tank has one.

Engine

CRITICAL COMPONENT! Once this goes down your engine will begin smoking and the tank will turn off. To restore this, you must repair the engine back to 100% and then turn over the tank again to start moving.

Tracks

Damaged tracks limit the mobility of the tank, however are not fully crucial. The tank can still move and spin fully but will remain stuck in first gear.

Tank Types

Understanding the different tank classifications and their roles on the battlefield.

Heavy Tank

The backbone of armored warfare. Heavy tanks provide superior armor protection and firepower, making them ideal for breakthrough operations and engaging enemy armor at long range.

Medium Tank

Balanced combination of mobility, armor, and firepower. Medium tanks excel at supporting infantry advances and providing mobile firepower across various terrain.

Light Tank

Fast and agile reconnaissance vehicles. Light tanks prioritize speed and mobility over armor, making them excellent for scouting, flanking, and hit-and-run tactics.

Recon Vehicle

Specialized reconnaissance and support vehicles. These units excel at gathering intelligence, providing fire support, and maintaining battlefield awareness. The spotter position can activate a recon ability to spot enemies in a large area.

Always Peek Cover

Position your tank so only the turret is visible (Hull down), or peek cover to where you can back up quickly.

  • Use terrain to better ricochet, as the higher you are the easier it is to bounce shots
  • Keep hull behind cover and only expose turret if possible
  • Use reverse to quickly escape if you get shot first

Survivability & Positioning

Never stay out in the open, always have an escape route.

  • Always be moving from cover to cover
  • Have a quick escape route by either quickly moving forward in first gear (especially with the panther) or quickly reversing (Tiger I, 76 Jumbo) depending on which gears work quicker on your respective tank
  • Ensure you always prioritize other armor, despite what blueberries run up and tell you. You cannot survive if armor is not subdued first
  • You have a big gun, use it for range. You cannot turn around fast enough in a fight, but you can the further away you are

Communication & Coordination

Master crew communication and team coordination for maximum combat effectiveness.

  • Maintain constant crew communication
  • Coordinate with other tank crews
  • Use proper radio protocols
  • Share intelligence and positions

Pro Tips from BEER Clan

  • Communication Tactics: Often times people will be overcommited to "legit" tank coms.. but the reality is this is a game and sometimes simple is better, especially for "new" tankers or tankers that you havent played with. Starting off with "gunner/driver/spotter" followed by a clock direction (12-front, 6- back etc). This will help with basic orientation. Driver commands can be called out as "ass back right" or "ass back left" for easy orientation. This tip is 100% up to you and whatever your crew jives with so be sure to see what works best for your crew!
  • Focus the Hull.. ALWAYS: Unlike War Thunder or Squad 44, Hell Let Loose uses simplified damage - focus on hitting the hull for maximum effectiveness
  • Engine Positioning: Keep engine compartment protected - rear hits can leave you stranded and unable to restart
  • How to Ricochet: Peak the front of your tank out of cover at a 30 degree angle to increase the likelihood of a ricochet. Your tank being higher than another tank can also increase this likelihood
  • The Smoke Trick: Use smoke for retreating or attacking, don't forget you can ping THROUGH the smoke! ‚ñ∂ Watch Demo
  • Retreat Timing: Know when to disengage - survival and repositioning beats dying for a single kill

How to be a Good Driver

more ‚ñº

Driving in Hell Let Loose, points and procedures from a pub dirtbag.

Good Teamwork as a Driver:

In our tank crews usually the tank commander is calling the shots and plays. As a driver it's critical to follow your TC's lead, ie; do not move the tank until you get the command under any normal circumstance. Doing so will make your gunner shank shots, TC lose vision or get disoriented, and likely get you killed. Fight the urge and trust your crew to do their jobs, at all costs. TC is the brains, gunner is the killer, and you get them all to the place to do their part. Listen to your TC.

In an absolute life or death emergency where you need to make a small maneuver or its lights out, call it out as soon as possible while performing it. "Angling tank right", "satchel close left" etc. Make the call outs as fast and as poignant as possible to not overload your other crew who are focusing on their own position demands, and are going to get freaked out by your abrupt movements. These positional changes should very rarely occur if ever.

Don't hesitate to make call outs as you see them to your team, but make them as short and accurate as possible. I like to rely on the compass marks as they're a universal reference point between all positions. "Tiger, top of the hill, 187" is far superior to "uhh tiger up there, north by the tree, do you see it?".

Driving:

It's been my experience that turning in reverse rather than 1st is typically far faster regardless of tank. So if the TC calls out "spin left, satchel guy!", I will typically reverse hard right. In these spins try to anticipate when your main gun will cover the target to stop without the gunner having to rotate the turret, which costs precious time and can make the difference.

Use the terrain to your turning advantage. The less tank track surface that is connected to ground, the tighter and smoother you can turn. Try it on different surfaces (garbage heaps in Carentan, berms on the side of roads and between hedge rows). Be aware though that you can straddle things like low walls and random shovels etc that will effectively leave you stuck and embarrassed if the tank is straddling it with no track-to-ground contact.

Try to hit all elevation changes at an angle. That is, always approach tall berms, hills, piles of crap at around a 30 to 45 degree angle. Doing so will maintain your rpm, speed, and help you avoid getting stuck on obstacles.

RPM, Speed, and Machine Gunning Nerds for the New Driver:

The tanks all have different turning radius, speeds, and acceleration. Jump in the practice server, or an empty server and really get comfortable with each. Focus on getting the feeling for turns in 1st particularly, in my opinion.

This might be controversial to some TC's, but I will use down shifting to control speed in certain situations. If we are charging a hard point and I notice the gunner is reloading a round, about to plaster a target if he can get the round in the chamber in time, I will downshift 1 to 2 gears momentarily to buy him the time. I would typically not drop below two gears total in any situation. You can find different scenarios where going from 4th to 3rd or 2nd, or 2nd to 1st will buy you an invaluable few seconds in the right situations, if you are aware of the current threats. This may freak out your TC.

Always keep your RPM at 2500/max. This is just a Hell Let Loose fact of life. If you do not, you will probably stall and have to downshift all the way to 1st and climb back up, possibly getting you killed in the process.

If you are losing rpm, downshift while keeping the throttle at max (holding W) until you get back to 2500, then upshift, wait a second or 2, and upshift again etc.

You have a machine gun, don't be afraid to use it. It's fun to rake enemies down when they least expect it from a hull mg. It can be a great infantry deterrent while your gunner is otherwise occupied. However it is best used in short bursts of 5 to 10 rounds. Veteran players will key in on sustained fire and use it, and your tracers, to locate you. This is even more important on night maps where your tracers look like laser beams.

The Old Quick Turn:

I'm not taking credit for this, I believe it was the TL guys who discovered it a few years ago. The Jumbo 76 has a unique attribute in that you can turn extremely fast on a fixed point. To do this you should already be in 4th gear at 2500 rpm. In one operation hit and hold either A,W, and downshift ( shift by default) or D,W and Shift until you drop to 2nd gear. As soon as you land in second gear immediately release only the shift key and you will effectively "spin on a dime". This is very useful for anti tank attacks from behind or to the sides. Afaik no other tank can do this reliably, although I have done it in a tiger once or twice, way back in the day.

If you miss the shift or are not at full rpm the entire maneuver, you will stall and have to go to first and start over, and probably die. So practice it, and don't gamble with it in the moment.

It helps to listen for 2 distinct shift click noises (clunk...clunk) before releasing the shift key, don't rely on the actual gear indicator on screen.

These are the most important things I have learned in carting Fresh and my fellow BEER homies around over the last 4 1/2 years. A lot of people see driving as some sort of a mundane necessity in HLL, but all of them know the value of a real driver when it goes wrong. Listen to your TC, make jokes, bang through the gears, and roast people with the machine gun. Smile, you bring the people to the party and the party to the people.
